# Example output — Community Harvest Food Bank (Jan–Jun 2026)

This is a model of good output for the sample CSV. It demonstrates tone and structure.
Always adapt to the real data; never reuse these numbers for a different file.

## Executive Summary

Over the first half of 2026, Community Harvest Food Bank met sharply rising community need.
Meals served grew +48% (12,400 → 18,400) and families served grew +42% (1,850 → 2,635),
with new clients nearly doubling (+97%). The organization delivered 91,050 meals across the
period on $319,700 in donations.

The defining challenge is supply, not service. Food inventory fell -29% (38,000 → 27,100 lbs)
even as demand surged — the central risk for the second half of the year. Donations stayed
ahead of operating costs every month, but grew more slowly than costs (+18% vs +24%), so the
monthly cushion is narrowing. Encouragingly, cost per meal *improved* from $2.91 to $2.43,
indicating the team is operating more efficiently as it scales.

## KPI Snapshot (June vs January)

- Meals served: 18,400 (+48.4%)
- Families served: 2,635 (+42.4%)
- Pantry visits: 3,425 (+42.7%)
- Donations: $57,100 (+18.5%)
- Food inventory: 27,100 lbs (-28.7%)
- Volunteer hours: 1,185 (+28.8%)

## Key Trends

- Demand rising fast: meals, families, and pantry visits all up 42–48% in six months.
- New clients nearly doubled (+97%) — growth in first-time need, not just repeat visits.
- Inventory declined every month (38,000 → 27,100 lbs) — the only major metric trending down.
- Donations up but trailing costs: +18.5% vs +24.1%, so the monthly surplus is shrinking.
- Efficiency improving: cost per meal fell from $2.91 to $2.43 despite higher volume.

## Risks & Concerns

- **High** — Rising demand against falling inventory; supply can't keep pace at current trajectory.
- **Medium** — Donations growing more slowly than operating costs. Not in deficit, but the cushion is thinning.

## Recommended Actions

1. Launch a targeted food drive now to rebuild inventory ahead of continued demand.
2. Ask donors for flexible / unrestricted operating support, not only food.
3. Recruit volunteers ahead of need rather than reacting to it.
4. Move inventory monitoring to a weekly cadence while the supply gap persists.

## Donor Update

in June — and 91,050 meals over the first half of the year. As more neighbors turn to us for
the first time, your continued support keeps shelves stocked and doors open. Right now, gifts
toward food and flexible operating support make the biggest difference.

## Board Summary

The organization is serving dramatically more people and doing so more efficiently per meal.
The decision-relevant issue is supply: inventory has fallen 29% while demand has risen 48%,
and the donation-to-cost cushion is narrowing. Recommended H2 focus: (1) food sourcing
strategy, (2) flexible funding, (3) volunteer capacity. No immediate financial distress, but
the supply gap warrants action before it becomes acute.

## Suggested Charts

- Demand vs. inventory (indexed line, Jan = 100) — shows the divergence; the clearest risk visual.
- Donations vs. operating cost (grouped bars by month) — shows the narrowing cushion.
- New vs. returning clients (stacked bars) — highlights the surge in first-time need.
